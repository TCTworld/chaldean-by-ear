Like a big portion of Chaldean first generation Americans, the language was simply not inherited; firstly, the language is extremely obscure and undocumented, with the only possibility of learning the language being through acquisition.

Unfortunately, I grew up in an environment where the only Chaldean people I knew was my family. It would be pretty hard to acquire a language that isnt taught to you and nobody speaks.

I love my grandparents. However, I simply don't know who they are. I don't have a personal connection with either of my grandparents that are still alive. My grandmother that recently passed away, who was my favorite person on earth, was also a victim of our language barrier. I never got to actually have a deep conversation with her, let alone any introspective altercation.

This is why I'm putting an effort into a program where you have a chance to apply the language in different ways and truly learn it. Thankfully there is an accurate source of information that teaches the language of Chaldean, which is the "Introductory Chaldean" book. through this book and two fluent speakers of the language, I compiled a full set of chapter coded flashcards that reveals an audio recording and visual representation of a specific word, where the student must type the word in english correctly. there is an option to reverse the term and definitions for your convenience.

My personal mission is to use this website that I created to try and earn the opportunity to get to know who my grandparents are, before it's too late.

Thank you,

Nate Kado
